CHAPTER I

THE LONG DAY

Clara was fourteen years old, but she worked exactly like a grown adult. She sat frozen at her heavy metal sewing machine for ten long hours every single day. The factory was a loud, hot, and incredibly crowded sweatshop.

The air inside the room was thick with flying pieces of white lint that made her cough and choke. Clara constantly watched the large clock hanging on the dirty brick wall. The black hands seemed to move so slowly, like they were stuck in molasses.

Her back ached from leaning over, and her fingers often bled from the fast needles. She was not allowed to talk to her friends or take a break to drink water. This miserable routine was her daily life. She wondered if things would ever change for girls like her.

THE DANGER

The shirtwaist factory was not just a hard place to work; it was a highly dangerous trap. Clara looked around and saw the tall, mean guards standing firmly by the exits.
